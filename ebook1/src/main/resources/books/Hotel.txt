If he had had his way, Peter McDermott thought, he would have fired the chief house detective long ago. But he had not 
had his way and now, once more, the obese ex-policeman was missing when he was needed most. McDermott leaned down from 
his husky six-and-a-half feet and jiggled the desk telephone impatiently. "Fifteen things break loose at once," he told 
the girl by the window of the wide, broadloomed office, "and nobody can find him." Christine Francis glanced at her wrist 
watch. It showed a few minutes before eleven p.m. "There's a bar on Baronne Street you might try." 
Peter McDermott nodded. "The switchboard's checking Ogilvie's hangouts."...
