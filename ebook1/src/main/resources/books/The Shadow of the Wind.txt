I still remember the day my father took me to the Cemetery of Forgotten Books for the first time. It was the early 
summer of 1945, and we walked through the streets of a Barcelona trapped beneath ashen skies as dawn poured over 
Rambla de Santa Monica in a wreath of liquid copper. 'Daniel, you mustn't tell anyone what you're about to see today,' 
my father warned. 'Not even your friend Tomas. No one.'...
